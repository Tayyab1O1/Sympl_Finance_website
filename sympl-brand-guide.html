03 · Illustration style

Hand-drawn warmth meets data clarity.

The most distinctive and ownable part of the visual identity. It blends a loose, hand-drawn feel with precise data-document accuracy - reflecting what Sympl does: make complex things feel human.

The pencil SVG filter

Hero illustrations are built in SVG then passed through a custom filter that applies fractal noise displacement + a subtle grain composite. Lines look slightly imprecise, as if sketched on paper. Reinforces "made by people, not machines."

Service mockup illustrations

The services section uses clean, polished UI mockups - rounded-rect "screens" with tables, charts, and status badges. No pencil filter. The contrast between rough hero art and precise service mockups creates visual hierarchy.

Subject matter

Concrete artefacts: ledgers, bar charts, checklists, pay stubs, forecasts. Secondary motifs: lighthouse (guidance), handshake (partnership), coin stack (finance). Avoid generic human figures.

Sparkles

Small four-pointed sparkles appear near primary illustrations. Always in Sorbet #FFB97D or Bay #65C1BF. Maximum two per scene - more reads as clip-art.

05 · Voice & tone

Knowledgeable colleague,
not consultant.

Plain language, first-person warmth, and occasional lightness - without being flippant about the work nonprofits do.

Plain, not dumbed-down

Explain "restricted funds" in two sentences without "fiduciary." The audience is smart - just not accountants.

Restricted grants are yours to spend only on what the funder specified.

Warm, not gushing

First-person, genuine investment in the mission - but no exclamation marks unless earned.

✅ "We're genuinely invested in your mission."
✗ "We absolutely love working with amazing organizations!"

Specific, not vague

Numbers and timelines build trust. Use them wherever possible.

✅ "Books closed within five business days."
✗ "We work quickly."

Confident, not salesy

CTAs are actions, not promises. Low-pressure framing always wins.

✅ "Book a free consultation."
✗ "Start your journey today!"

08 · Social content

Four weeks, one arc.

The launch plan follows a deliberate narrative: introduce → build authority → show value → convert. Cadence is 3 posts per week on each platform.

Content arc
WeekThemeGoal
1Launch & introduceBrand awareness
2Establish authorityTrust and credibility
3Show the valueProblem → solution
4Engage & convertLead generation
LinkedIn - priority channel

Long-form text with 3–5 structured points. Native polls (no graphic). Educational carousels (6 slides). Testimonial cards. Link always in first comment, not body copy.

Instagram - visual channel

Short copy, emoji-forward. Reels for step-by-step tips. Carousels with "save + share" prompts. Stories mirror feed polls. Link in bio - update monthly for campaigns.
