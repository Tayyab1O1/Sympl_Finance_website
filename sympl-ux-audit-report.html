Executive summary

The Sympl Finance site is visually polished, fast, and structurally responsive — no page shows layout breakage or horizontal scroll at any width from 375px to 1440px, page weight is exceptionally light (~130 KB of images on the homepage), and the design system is applied consistently across all 13 pages. The serious problems are not cosmetic. They sit at the two points where the site is supposed to do its job: capturing leads and letting every visitor reach the service pages.

The three things to internalize

1 · Every lead-capture form on the site is a dead end. Fix Required ✓ Resolved

The contact form, the 3-step quote wizard, and the homepage checklist signup all show a success message ("Thanks! We'll be in touch within one business day") without sending data anywhere — there is no backend, no email service, no network request. A prospective client who fills in the quote wizard believes Sympl has their information; Sympl never receives it. Until this is fixed, every marketing dollar and SEO win driving traffic to these pages produces silently lost leads and, worse, prospects who conclude Sympl ignored them.

2 · On touch tablets and for keyboard users, the Services and Who We Serve menus cannot be opened. Fix Required ✓ Resolved

Between 901px and desktop widths the header shows hover-only dropdowns, and the site's JavaScript actively cancels clicks on the "Services ▾" and "Who We Serve ▾" triggers. On an iPad in landscape (1024px) tapping them does nothing — verified in testing — so five core pages (Bookkeeping, Payroll, CFO & Advisory, Arts, Settlement) are unreachable from the header. Keyboard users hit the same wall: focus never reveals the dropdown, and the script even blurs the link after clicking. This is both a conversion leak and a WCAG 2.1.1 (Keyboard) failure.

3 · Internal placeholder content is live in production. Fix Required ✓ Resolved ◔ Testimonials to be added

The Clients & Partners page displays an editor's note to the team — "Use this as the flexible client list. Swap placeholder marks for approved logos and add real client names as permissions…" — as visible page copy. The homepage social-proof section carries similar draft text, and several testimonials are attributed to generic personas ("Gallery client", "Festival client", "Program lead"). For a firm selling trust and accuracy, shipped draft copy directly undercuts the brand promise.

Beyond these, the audit found a fully invisible button (1.0:1 contrast) on three pages caused by a missing shared CSS rule, a set of brand-color text styles that sit just below WCAG AA contrast thresholds, sub-44px touch targets in the header and footer, hotlinked images served from Squarespace and Unsplash accounts the team doesn't control, no analytics of any kind, and no privacy policy behind the footer's "Privacy" label — a gap for a financial-services firm collecting personal data through forms. Details, evidence, and fixes follow.

Site-wide findings

These issues repeat across pages because they live in shared components (sympl-components.js, sympl-components.css) or shared patterns. Fixing each once fixes it everywhere. Use the filter to focus on a severity level.

Forms simulate success but never transmit data Fix Required ✓ Resolved

Why it matters: this is the site's primary conversion path. All three capture points fail identically: the handler calls e.preventDefault() and swaps in a success message. Network tracing during test submissions confirmed zero outbound requests. The quote wizard compounds it — a user invests effort across 3 steps and 18 fields before the data evaporates.

Evidence
sympl-contact.html:361 — submit handler only toggles #form-success and disables the button.
sympl-quote.html:544 — submitQuote() hides the steps and shows #quote-success; nothing else.
index.html:3106 — lead magnet handler carries the comment "front-end only; wire to ESP later".
Browser network log after submitting each form: no POST/fetch/XHR issued.

Fix: wire all three forms to a real endpoint — a Vercel serverless function forwarding to email/CRM, or a managed service (Formspree, Basin, Netlify Forms). Add a loading state, a genuine failure state ("Something went wrong — email us at contact@symplsolutions.com"), and keep the existing success UI.

✓ Resolved July 7, 2026 — all three forms now submit via FormSubmit to tayyab@symplsolutions.com (CC natasha@symplsolutions.com; subjects "Webform inquiry from website" / "Quote inquiry from website") through a shared symplSendInquiry() helper, with loading, success-on-delivery, and error-with-mailto-fallback states. Pending: one-time FormSubmit activation email click after first live submission.

No validation on any form — empty submissions "succeed" Fix Required ✓ Resolved

Why it matters: not a single field on the contact form or quote wizard carries required, and clicking submit with every field empty immediately shows the thank-you state (verified). Once the forms are wired to a backend, this becomes a garbage-data problem; today it deepens the false-success problem. The quote wizard also lets users advance through all steps without entering anything, so its stepper communicates progress that isn't real.

Evidence
Empty submit on /sympl-contact → button flips to "Sent ✓", success banner shows.
All 18 quote-wizard inputs report required: false; goStep() performs no checks.
Only the homepage email field has a minimal includes("@") check.

Fix: add required to name/email (contact) and email (quote step 3), validate per-step in goStep(), and show inline error messages tied to fields with aria-describedby. Native HTML validation gets 80% of this for free.

✓ Resolved July 7, 2026 — required added to contact name/email and quote name/org/email; the wizard's goStep() now blocks forward navigation until the current step validates; all forms use native browser validation messages. Verified: empty submissions are blocked on every form.

Dropdown navigation is hover-only; taps and keyboard are locked out Fix Required ✓ Resolved

Why it matters: the drawer only appears at ≤900px. Above that — iPads in landscape (1024px), Surface devices, touch laptops — the desktop nav renders, its dropdowns open on :hover only, and sympl-components.js:383–390 cancels clicks on both triggers (preventDefault() + blur()). Testing at 1024px: tapping "Services ▾" produces no visible change. Keyboard focus likewise never opens the menu, and there are no aria-haspopup/aria-expanded attributes. Five revenue pages become unreachable from the header for these users (footer links are the only path). WCAG 2.1.1 Keyboard failure.

Evidence
At 1024×768: hamburger hidden; .dropdown computed style stays opacity:0 / visibility:hidden after both click and focus on the trigger.
Trigger href is #services but navigation is suppressed by the click handler.

Fix: make triggers real toggles: on click/Enter, toggle an .open class and aria-expanded; keep hover as an enhancement. Add :focus-within to the dropdown CSS so tabbing into the menu keeps it open. Alternatively, lower the drawer breakpoint's replacement — show the hamburger up to ~1080px.

✓ Resolved July 7, 2026 — triggers now toggle on click/tap/Enter with aria-haspopup/aria-expanded, only one menu opens at a time, and outside-click and Escape close them. Hover behavior is preserved for mouse users. Verified at 1024px.

"See services" button is invisible (1.0:1 contrast) on three pages Fix Required ✓ Resolved

Why it matters: the shared <sympl-final-cta> component renders a dark-navy band with a "See services" ghost button styled by .btn-ghost.rev. That rule lives in per-page CSS — and About, Clients & Partners, and Blog-post never define it. Computed styles on those pages: text rgb(0,34,54) on background rgb(0,34,54) — a contrast ratio of exactly 1.0. The secondary CTA in the site's strongest closing section is literally unreadable there.

Evidence
grep 'btn-ghost.rev{': rule present in index, bookkeeping, payroll, cfo-advisory, arts, settlement, nonprofit, blog — absent in sympl-about.html, sympl-clients-partners.html, sympl-blog-post.html.
Live inspection on /sympl-clients-partners: color: rgb(0,34,54), background: transparent, band bg rgb(0,34,54).

Fix: move the .btn-ghost.rev rule (and its hover state) into sympl-components.css next to the component that requires it, then delete the per-page copies. One-line prevention of an entire class of drift bugs.

✓ Resolved July 7, 2026 — .btn-ghost.rev now lives in sympl-components.css. Verified on /sympl-clients-partners: the button renders cream on navy (≈13:1 contrast) on all pages using the shared final-CTA component.

Draft/internal copy and placeholder testimonials are live Fix Required ✓ Resolved ◔ Testimonials to be added

Why it matters: visitors on the Clients & Partners page can read an instruction meant for the Sympl team. The homepage proof section leads with "Swap in real logos and client photos as they arrive." Several testimonials use anonymous placeholder personas alongside real ones, which invites doubt about the real ones too. Social proof is the core persuasion device for a services firm — draft-quality proof reads as fabricated proof.

The anonymous placeholder testimonials on the Arts and Settlement pages ("Gallery client", "Festival client", "Settlement client", "Program lead", "Finance committee chair") are intentionally left as-is — client confirmed real testimonials for these sectors will be swapped in directly, tracked as a content to-do rather than an open bug.

No privacy policy — footer "Privacy" is a dead label Fix Required ✓ Resolved

Why it matters: the footer renders "Privacy" as a plain <span> with no page behind it (sympl-components.js:368). The site collects names, emails, phone numbers, and organizational financial details through its forms. For a finance firm serving Ontario nonprofits, PIPEDA expectations and basic client trust both require a real privacy policy — and its absence is most visible at the exact moment someone is deciding whether to hand over their information.

Fix: publish a privacy policy page and link it from the footer. Include what's collected via forms, where it's stored, and a contact for data questions.

✓ Resolved July 7, 2026 — /sympl-privacy published (PIPEDA-aware: data collected per form, FormSubmit relay, hosting/fonts disclosure, retention, access/correction/withdrawal rights) and the footer "Privacy" label is now a real link on every page. Pending: legal review of the draft.

Brand teal and amber text colors sit just below AA contrast Recommended

Why it matters: the teal kickers/eyebrows (rgb(0,126,124) on cream) measure 4.24–4.29:1 — just under the 4.5:1 requirement for their 12–13px size. The amber "104%" stat chip measures 3.72:1 and client-sector tags 4.1:1 at 11px. These are recurring design tokens, so a small color adjustment fixes dozens of instances at once. Low-vision users and anyone on a dim mobile screen in daylight are the ones squinting.

Evidence (computed, per element)
p.kicker 4.29:1 @13px (needs 4.5) — every page
b.chip-warn "104%" 3.72:1 @13px — homepage
span.ptag 4.1:1 @12px — arts; span.client-sector 4.24:1 @11px — clients page

Fix: darken the teal token to ≈#00706E and the amber to ≈#8a5a00 (both pass 4.5:1 on the cream background), or bump these labels to ≥18.66px bold where the 3:1 large-text threshold applies.

Touch targets below the 44px guideline in header, footer, and inline links Recommended

Why it matters: measured at 375px: the hamburger button is 38×32px, all footer links 30px tall, "→ more" links on the homepage 17–22px tall, and blog filter chips 32px. Apple/WCAG 2.5.8 guidance is 44×44. Small targets cause mis-taps precisely on the highest-intent elements (menu, service links). The mobile drawer gets this right — its links measure 279×44+ — so the pattern already exists in the codebase.

Fix: add padding (not font-size) — hamburger to 44×44, footer links padding:10px 0, inline "more" links padding:12px 0; display:inline-block.

Team photos and blog imagery hotlinked from third-party accounts Recommended

Why it matters: the four About-page team headshots load from images.squarespace-cdn.com — the firm's old Squarespace account. If that subscription lapses or the media library is cleaned, the About page loses its faces silently. Roughly 18 blog/homepage images load from images.unsplash.com at request time, adding third-party requests to every visit and using generic stock that weakens an otherwise personal brand.

Evidence
Network trace on /sympl-about: 4 squarespace-cdn requests (currently 200 OK, lazy-loaded).
grep counts: sympl-blog.html ×18, sympl-blog-data.js ×18, index.html ×5 unsplash references.

Fix: download the four headshots into images/Team/ as webp (matching every other image on the site) this week. Migrate blog images to local copies over time, replacing the most generic ones with real client/work photography as it arrives.

No analytics — the site's performance is unmeasurable Recommended

Why it matters: no page includes any analytics (checked for GA4, GTM, Plausible, Fathom, Umami). Nobody can currently answer "which pages convert," "where do mobile users drop off," or even "did traffic grow" — and once the forms are fixed, there will be no funnel data proving it. Given the audience (nonprofit EDs wary of tracking), a lightweight privacy-first option is also a brand fit.

Fix: add Plausible or Fathom (single script, no cookie banner needed) with custom events on form submissions and CTA clicks. Pairs with the new privacy policy.

Keyboard & screen-reader gaps: no skip link, no drawer focus management, missing landmarks Recommended

Why it matters: (a) no page has a skip-to-content link, so keyboard users tab through the full nav on every page; (b) opening the mobile drawer leaves focus on the page behind it — focus never moves in, isn't trapped, and the aria-hidden page is still tabbable (Escape does work, which is good); (c) index, arts, settlement and nonprofit lack a <main> landmark; (d) footer columns use <h4>, creating an h2→h4 skip on every page; (e) the homepage's interactive "Click me" dashboard toggles are plain <span>s — invisible to keyboards entirely.

Fix: add a skip link in the shared nav; on drawer open, move focus to the close button and trap Tab within; wrap page content in <main> on the four pages missing it; restyle footer headings as styled <p>/<div>s; convert the hero toggles to <button>s.

Form inputs under 16px trigger iOS auto-zoom Recommended

Why it matters: contact and quote inputs render at 14.5px, the homepage newsletter field at 13px. iOS Safari zooms the whole page when focusing any input under 16px, disorienting the user mid-form — a classic silent conversion killer on the device class most nonprofit EDs use after hours. The newsletter input is also only ~101px wide at 375px, cramped for a full email address.

Fix: set input, select, textarea { font-size: 16px } at mobile widths; let the newsletter field flex to full width on small screens.

Social sharing image is an SVG logo — previews will break Recommended

Why it matters: every page's og:image/twitter:image points to Dark.svg. LinkedIn, Slack, iMessage and X do not render SVG preview images — shares of the site will show a blank card. For a firm whose referrals travel through email and LinkedIn, that's lost credibility at the exact moment of word-of-mouth.

Fix: export a 1200×630 PNG/JPG social card (logo + tagline on brand cream) and reference it absolutely on all pages.

Unbranded default 404 page Recommended

Why it matters: a mistyped or stale URL currently lands on the hosting platform's default error page — no logo, no navigation, no way back. Broken inbound links (old Squarespace URLs, typo'd newsletter links) become hard exits.

Fix: add a branded 404.html (Vercel picks it up automatically) with the site nav and links to Services, Blog, and Contact.

Testimonial carousel: arrows only, no swipe or position indicator Minor / Consider

The carousel has well-sized 52×52 arrows with aria-labels (good), but no touch/drag support and no dots, so mobile users can't tell there are 3 cards or swipe as they'd expect. Arrows correctly disable at the ends.

Fix: simplest robust option — switch the track to CSS overflow-x:auto + scroll-snap, keep arrows as an enhancement; add dots or a "1 / 3" counter.

Blog filters lack toggle semantics; card grid works well otherwise Minor / Consider

Filter chips are real <button>s (good) and filtering works (18 → 2 posts verified), with an aria-live "no results" region. But the active filter isn't conveyed to assistive tech (aria-pressed is absent) and result-count changes aren't announced. No search across 18 posts yet — fine today, worth adding as the library grows.

Fix: add aria-pressed to chips and announce "N articles shown" in the existing live region.

FAQ open-state can clip after viewport changes Minor / Consider

FAQ accordions are semantically solid (buttons + aria-expanded, verified working). But the answer height is fixed to scrollHeight px at click time — rotate the phone or resize and an open answer can clip its last line.

Fix: recompute open-item max-height on resize, or use grid-template-rows: 0fr/1fr transitions instead of pixel max-height.

Decorative dashboard mock-ups contain 8–11px text Minor / Consider

The hero/report visualizations use labels down to 8px ("actual", "budget") and 10.5px ("Cash flow forecast"). As decorative illustrations this is acceptable, but they present real-looking information users will try to read, and team-role labels (11px) and the CPA note (11.5px) are genuine content.

Fix: raise genuine content labels to ≥12px; mark purely decorative chart text with aria-hidden.

Blog-post error state leaves the tab titled "Loading…" Minor / Consider

Bad article IDs show a proper "Article not found" message with a Back-to-Blog link (good pattern), but document.title stays "Loading… | Sympl Finance". Dynamic posts also lack canonical URLs, which slightly weakens SEO for the content marketing engine.

Fix: set the title in the not-found branch; inject <link rel="canonical"> per post.

Strengths & patterns worth protecting

A large share of this site is done well above the standard for a small-firm marketing site. When making the fixes above, protect these:

Exceptional page weightHomepage ships ~130 KB of images across 36 images, webp everywhere local, no single request over 100 KB, subset Google Fonts. Most agencies ship 10× this.
Zero layout breakageEvery page tested at 375/768/1024/1440px with no horizontal overflow and sensible stacking — the 900px/620px breakpoint system is disciplined.
Excellent mobile drawer44px+ targets throughout, Escape-to-close, scroll lock, aria-hidden toggling. Add focus trapping and it's exemplary.
Correct FAQ semanticsReal buttons, working aria-expanded, 85px tap targets. Copy quality inside the answers is a differentiator too.
Complete meta layerUnique titles, meta descriptions, and OG tags on all pages; sitemap + robots.txt present; internal pages correctly noindexed.
Reduced-motion supportprefers-reduced-motion honored in shared CSS and page animations — rare attention to vestibular accessibility.
Universal alt textZero images missing alt attributes across all 13 pages; decorative initials-avatars correctly aria-hidden.
Handled error statesBad blog IDs get a friendly not-found message with a route back — a pattern many production sites lack.
Component reuseShared web components (nav, footer, proof, FAQ, CTA) keep 13 pages consistent — the .rev bug shows the one place per-page CSS escaped this system.
Copy specificitySector pages speak the audience's exact language (T3010, IRCC claim periods, T4A classification, salary allocation) — the site's biggest conversion asset once the forms work.

Methodology. All 13 public pages were loaded in a Chromium browser against the local build (commit be08ae1) at 375, 768, 1024, and 1440px viewports. Findings derive from: computed-style contrast measurement (WCAG 2.1 formulas) on rendered text; bounding-box measurement of interactive elements; live interaction testing (drawer, dropdowns, FAQ, carousel, blog filters, all three forms including empty-submission tests); network request tracing; and source review of shared components.
